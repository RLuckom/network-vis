What Happens on an Interface?

What you're looking at is a hive plot showing up to 329 NetFlow records I collected on my WLAN0 interface while I was browsing the internet.

The axis at left is a log scale of the ports the requests originated from. At the very bottom, you can see a few requests that started at port 0, then a cluster at port 53, another at port 80, and one at port 443. The central axis holds the nodes representing the individual flows themselves spaced proportionally by time; if you hover over them you can see full details in the 'Currently Selected' box. The axis at the right shows the destination ports of the flows.

At this point you might wonder why data is originating on ports 53, 80, and 443. In fact, those clusters represent incoming flows which originated on those ports on servers on the web in response to requests from my computer. To see only requests coming from my computer, enter 192.168.1.2 in the 'Source IP' filter box. Now you should see only flows that went from my computer out to the internet. To see what came the other way, delete my IP from the source box and put it in the destination box. Each of those boxes accepts partial IPs and regular expressions, so you can filter based on subnets as well as full addresses.
